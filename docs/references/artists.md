# references — artists

Lesser-known and canonical references that inform the Swiss × Japan direction. Pull at least 10 images per artist into the moodboard in phase 2.

## Canonical (the lineage)

### Ikko Tanaka (1930–2002)

Japanese graphic designer who studied Swiss grids. The bridge. Posters for Noh theatre, Mazda, Issey Miyake. Grid + restraint + Japanese sensitivity to negative space.

- Search: *Ikko Tanaka Noh poster*, *Ikko Tanaka Nihon Buyo*

### Wim Crouwel (1928–2019)

Swiss-trained Dutch designer. The grid as the entire design. Stedelijk Museum catalogues.

- Reference: *New Alphabet*, Stedelijk Museum work

### Karel Martens (b. 1939)

Dutch typographer and letterpress artist. Color as event, restraint everywhere else. The OASE journal covers are the syllabus.

- Reference: OASE architecture journal, *Printed Matter* monograph

## Lesser-known (the real moodboard)

### Manuel Raeder

Berlin-based studio. Books for artists. One typographic gesture per cover, surrounded by silence.

- manuelraeder.co.uk

### Na Kim

Korean designer, NYC. Worked at MoMA, Apartamento. Quiet color blocks, generous space, type with weight.

- na-kim.com

### Yugo Nakamura (tha.jp)

Japanese designer / programmer. Motion as content. His site is almost no UI chrome — direct intersection of code, motion, and ma.

- tha.jp

### Hassan Rahim

LA-based art director (formerly Calvin Klein, Apple). Image-led, dark restraint, type used like a stamp.

- hassanrahim.com

### Tezzo Suzuki

Japanese product + graphic designer. Cross-medium minimalism. His own site is a study in ma.

- tezzosuzuki.com

### Folch (Folch Studio)

Barcelona. Editorial work for fashion and culture brands. Magazine sensibility translated to digital with rare discipline.

- folchstudio.com

### Daniel Eatock

British conceptual designer. One-page websites that are one idea. Self-portraits, books with single rules.

- eatock.com

### Bibliothèque Design

London studio. Identity and editorial work. Quiet but technically perfect typography.

- bibliothequedesign.com

### Studio Frith

London. Editorial design with restraint and a real interest in materials.

- studiofrith.com

### Mevis & Van Deursen

Dutch design studio. Conceptual restraint, weird-but-disciplined typography. Books for art institutions.

- Search: *Mevis Van Deursen Stedelijk*, *Mevis Van Deursen Casco*

## Photography / image-makers (for film-page mood)

### Rinko Kawauchi

Japanese photographer. Light, breath, mundane objects made luminous. Sets the register for film poster frames.

### Hiroshi Sugimoto

Japanese photographer. Long-exposure seascapes and theatre interiors. Empty centres.

### Wolfgang Tillmans

German photographer. Quiet, intimate, image as object. His own published books are a layout reference.

## How to use this list

In phase 2:

1. For each artist, save 8–12 images to `docs/moodboard/artists/<name>/`
2. Annotate each with one sentence: *what specifically does this teach echo?*
3. Tag images by the principle they demonstrate: `grid`, `ma`, `typography`, `color-event`, `restraint`, `image-led`, `motion`

The point isn't to copy. It's to internalize the **register** so the sketch lands on the same emotional frequency.

## What we are not pulling from

- Awwwards / Site of the Day winners
- "Modern minimal SaaS" landing pages
- Linear / Stripe / Vercel
- Any landing page that ranks for "minimalist portfolio template"
- Apple's marketing site (too commercial-clean, wrong register)

These are good design but the wrong reference for this project.
