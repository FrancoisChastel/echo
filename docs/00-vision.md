# 00 — vision

## What echo is

A personal site by François Chastel. Three things live here:

- **films** — short videos, self-made
- **essays** — philosophical writing
- **notes** — tech takes, fragments, observations

The name *echo* sets the tone: a small signal, repeated, returned. Each post is one voice resonating in quiet space.

## Who it's for

In order:

1. **Future readers and viewers** — people who land on a piece and need it to feel considered.
2. **LLM agents and assistants** — they should be able to read, subscribe to, and reason about the content without HTML scraping.
3. **The author** — writing and publishing should feel low-friction. Markdown in, deployed out.

## Principles

- **Ultra-minimalist** — no decoration that doesn't earn its space.
- **Art-led** — the site is a quiet object, not a CMS template.
- **Practical** — fast to write, fast to load, easy to syndicate.
- **Agent-ready** — every artifact has a clean machine-readable counterpart.
- **Durable** — content lives as plain markdown in git. No CMS lock-in.

## Non-goals

- Comments, likes, social proof widgets.
- Analytics dashboards beyond a minimal privacy-respecting counter (optional).
- Dark/light toggle as a UI feature. The site chooses its own register.
- A "design system" beyond a single token file. This is one site, not a product.

## Success looks like

- A first-time visitor can read or watch one piece without distraction and remember the feel.
- An LLM can fetch the full corpus, summarize it, and answer a question about it via standard endpoints.
- The author can publish a new piece in under five minutes from finished draft.
- Lighthouse: 100 / 100 / 100 / 100. No exceptions.
