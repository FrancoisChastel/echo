Field notes, vol. 3

2026.05.12 · 1:30 · 1920×1080

A 90-second study of light through paper. Shot over an afternoon in February in a room with one window and a single sheet of tracing paper. The camera barely moves.

I started this series to learn what a film could be when stripped of plot, dialogue, and movement — when the only material is light and time. The third entry is the quietest so far. There is a moment near the middle where the shadow of a hand crosses the frame and I almost cut it; I left it because the hand is doing what the camera is doing, which is watching.

Shot on a Sony A7C, 35mm prime, no rig. Color graded warm to match the afternoon. Music: silence.
